12 - Street Name and Address Discrepancy

5707 Scenic Ave - Street Name / Address Discrepancy

Current hypothesis

The discrepancy is likely a combination of:

  1. street-name changes (Center St → Railroad Street → Scenic Avenue)
  2. later address renumbering (5355 → 5707)

New evidence

1854 and 1867 map evidence

  • 1854 historic Oswego County map appears to show the road as Center St.
  • 1867 review still appears to show Center St.

1906 / 1911 Sanborn evidence

Manual review indicates the candidate road aligned with the Skinner-house location appears as Railroad in the 1906/1911 Sanborn context, while Centre appears to be a different intersecting road.

Historical society page text now captured

A public Mexico Historical Society page reproducing material credited to the August 28, 1942 Mexico Independent preserves the street-history language directly, not just as a search-result snippet.

"Scenic avenue has had three names. Several years it was renamed Scenic in place of Railroad street. In the village’s early history, the street was called Center street and it did not extend to the present railroad crossing which was built in 1868 when the first train passed thru Mexico."

The same page also notes that at one point Railroad street did not yet extend that far north from the center of the village, which helps explain how older references can look geographically inconsistent when compared with the modern street grid.

This is now the strongest public-web statement in hand supporting a three-stage street-name history.

1961 centennial issue bridge

The Mexico Independent Centennial Issue (1861-1961) includes a historical line referring to:

> "Main Street and Center Avenue (Scenic)"

That is an unusually valuable bridge because it explicitly ties the older Center naming to Scenic in a later retrospective source.

Evidence stack now

  • NRHP nomination (1991 filing) uses 5355 Scenic Avenue
  • present county and listing records use 5707 Scenic Ave
  • a newspaper snippet from the Mexico Independent, 24 June 1971 references Roy D. Matterson, 5355 Scenic Ave., Mexico, N.Y.
  • no useful modern public-web traces exist for 5355 Scenic Ave in Mexico
  • older map review suggests the road name evolved from Center St to Railroad Street to Scenic Avenue
  • 5707 matches the physical / historical / American Legion profile of the Skinner House

Best explanation so far

The house likely stayed the same while the address evolved in at least three stages:

  • older road name: Center St
  • later road name: Railroad Street
  • later road name: Scenic Avenue
  • by at least 1971, the address 5355 Scenic Ave was in active public use
  • later 911 / municipal renumbering: 5355 Scenic Ave → 5707 Scenic Ave

What would move this from strong theory to near-proof

  • a second historic map from a later year showing the same road as Scenic
  • village/town minutes or plat showing a Center → Scenic rename
  • E‑911 record or clerk confirmation of 5355 → 5707 renumbering
  • deed or tax record showing the same parcel under both addresses across time

Practical significance

This is increasingly looking less like “wrong house” and more like “same house, evolving address identity over decades.”

20th-century corroboration (family memorial)

The memorial for Dr. Avery Warner Skinner (son of Timothy W.) states he was “born … in the historic brick house on Railroad Street” in Mexico, NY. This is consistent with the documented street-name sequence (Center → Railroad → Scenic) and supports that the same house later mapped under Scenic Avenue numbering.
