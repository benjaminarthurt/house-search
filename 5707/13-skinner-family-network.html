13 - Skinner Family Network

5707 Scenic Ave - Skinner Family Network

Why this note exists

This note organizes the broader Skinner-family context around the house, the Village/Town of Mexico, and the public offices/business roles that make the family's link to a prominent village residence plausible.

Core family line currently supported

Timothy Skinner and Ruth (Warner) Skinner

Public biographical sources identify Timothy Skinner and Ruth Warner Skinner as the parents of Avery Skinner.

Avery Skinner (1796-1876)

Supported by public biographical material:

  • born in New Hampshire
  • moved to Watertown, then to the Town of Mexico area
  • founded / named Union Square (later Maple View)
  • built and kept an inn there
  • Oswego County treasurer
  • associate judge
  • state assemblyman
  • state senator
  • long-time postmaster
  • one of the most prominent Skinners in the Mexico/Oswego story

Timothy W. Skinner (1827-1899? late-life records active in 1897)

Supported by nomination narrative + clippings:

  • son of Avery and Elizabeth Huntington Skinner
  • raised in Maple View / Union Square context
  • settled in Mexico Village in 1853
  • admitted to the bar in 1857
  • joined Judge Cyrus Whitney in Whitney & Skinner
  • served as surrogate, village president, and Mexico Academy trustee
  • built the house later listed as the Timothy Skinner House nomination subject
  • later evidence shows financial distress / insolvency by 1897

Avery W. Skinner

Nomination and later clipping evidence indicate:

  • son of Timothy W. Skinner
  • became a teacher and later principal of Mexico Academy
  • a 1961 centennial issue states the American Legion bought in 1944 the former home of A. W. Skinner on Scenic Avenue

This is one of the strongest continuity bridges in the entire file.

Important associated names

Elizabeth Huntington Skinner

  • mother of Timothy W. Skinner per nomination narrative
  • wife of Avery Skinner

Cyrus Whitney

  • judge / lawyer / banking associate in Whitney & Skinner
  • 1874 mortgage-book evidence independently ties Whitney and Timothy W. Skinner together
  • important because this corroborates the nomination's professional biography for Timothy

Charles R. Skinner

  • identified in public Avery Skinner biography as a son from Avery's later marriage
  • notable public figure in his own right
  • relevant to the broader prominence of the Skinner family in the Mexico / Oswego sphere

House relationship summary

What is strongly supported now:

  • Timothy W. Skinner is the historically documented original owner / builder in the nomination narrative
  • Avery W. Skinner appears to be the later Skinner family member directly linked to the Scenic Avenue home before the Legion acquired it
  • the American Legion property history strongly overlaps the same house identity later associated with 5707 Scenic Ave

What is still worth finding

  • direct obituary or probate material for Timothy W. Skinner
  • direct biographical references to Avery W. Skinner and his residence
  • clearer family-tree links that anchor the house from Timothy W. Skinner to A. W. Skinner to Legion ownership
  • any local-history writing naming the house under both Skinner generations
