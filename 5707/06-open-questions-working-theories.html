06 - Open Questions & Working Theories

5707 Scenic Ave - Open Questions & Working Theories

Important note

This file is intentionally for non-final interpretations. Do not treat anything here as verified until backed by a record, inspection, or direct confirmation.

Working theory 1: 5707 is the Skinner House under a newer address

Why it seems plausible:

  • matching photos per user observation
  • same street
  • historic brick house profile fits
  • county record year built 1900 is close enough to the historical-era story to keep the theory alive

What would confirm it:

  • SHPO confirmation
  • nomination PDF / legal description match
  • town renumbering history
  • old map / plat chain showing road-name evolution from Center to Scenic Avenue

Working theory 2: Alessio intended a flip or quick reposition

Why it seems plausible:

  • bought Sept 2024, reselling quickly

What would support it:

  • permit activity
  • cosmetic updates only
  • similar buy/sell pattern on other properties

What would weaken it:

  • evidence of personal-life-driven sale
  • no investor pattern

Working theory 3: the house has a hidden issue that made the quick resale unattractive to keep

Possible categories:

  • roof / water / masonry
  • heating / utility cost surprise
  • basement moisture
  • title / zoning / 2-family-use complication
  • historic restriction surprise

What would support it:

  • incomplete or absent permit history
  • agent evasiveness
  • inspection findings
  • price cuts / listing stagnation

Working theory 4: the 2-family classification may be strategically useful or administratively stale

Possibilities:

  • legal 2-family with upside
  • formerly legal 2-family but now marketed as large single-family
  • stale assessor classification
  • hidden complication for insurance / lending / occupancy

What would resolve it:

  • assessor/town clarification
  • floor plan / separate access review
  • listing disclosures

Best current stance

Stay interested, but act like this is an old, potentially historic, recently turned-over property where the real value question will be answered by:

  1. historic-status confirmation
  2. permit history
  3. title
  4. inspection
