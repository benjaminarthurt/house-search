National Register of Historic Places · Mexico, NY

The Timothy Skinner House

A large brick Italianate residence at 5707 Scenic Ave (formerly Railroad Street), Mexico, Oswego County, NY — built c. 1850 by Timothy Warner Skinner and listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1991. This archive documents the house, its history, and the Skinner family.

NRHP Listed 1991Built c. 1850Italianate brickSkinner familyMexico, NY

The House

A prominent Italianate brick residence built by Timothy Warner Skinner — attorney, Oswego County Surrogate, village president, and prominent community figure — on what was then Railroad Street.

The Skinner Family

Three generations of the Skinner family are documented: Timothy W. Skinner (1827–1915), his son Dr. Avery Warner Skinner (1870–1937), and their descendants, with portraits and genealogical records.

NRHP & Preservation

The property was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1991 (ref 91000526) under the Mexico Village Multiple Property Submission. The full nomination is available in the archive.

American Legion Era

After the Skinner family era ended, the house passed to the American Legion in the early 1960s — a documented transition preserved in local newspaper records from the 1961 centennial issue.
