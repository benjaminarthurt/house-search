11 - Historic House Confirmation Hunt

5707 Scenic Ave - Historic House Confirmation Hunt

Goal

Move the Skinner/NRHP theory from strongly plausible to document-backed.

Strongest evidence currently in hand

  1. Timothy Skinner House is publicly described as:
  • NRHP-listed in 1991
  • large brick Italianate residence
  • later sold to the American Legion in 1964
  1. 5707 Scenic Ave county chain includes American Legion ownership before the 2006 sale.
  2. User has observed that the public-facing 5355 Scenic Ave address appears wrong/stale and that photos tied to it match 5707.
  3. The physical and historical profile of 5707 is consistent with the Skinner House thread.
  4. New map evidence and later review suggest the street name changed over time (Center / Railroad / Scenic).
  5. Newspaper evidence from Mexico Independent, 11 August 1897 references Timothy W. Skinner and his dwelling in the village of Mexico, directly supporting that he had a substantial residence in the village consistent with the nomination narrative.

New useful family-history findings

Avery Skinner line

Public sources consistently support:

  • Avery Skinner (1796-1876) was a major local political/civic figure
  • he founded/anchored Union Square (later Maple View)
  • he served in county and state office
  • the Skinner family remained prominent in the Mexico area

Timothy W. Skinner line

Public snippet-level evidence indicates:

  • Timothy W. Skinner was born in Mexico in 1827
  • admitted to the bar in Watertown in 1857
  • practiced law in Mexico village
  • was connected to the banking house of Whitney & Skinner
  • an 1874 mortgage-book snippet independently ties Timothy W. Skinner to Cyrus Whitney, reinforcing the Whitney & Skinner relationship described in the nomination narrative
  • an 1897 Supreme Court / assignee notice identifies Timothy W. Skinner as an insolvent debtor, adding an important late-life biographical development that may matter for the property's later history
  • a 1961 centennial issue clipping states that the American Legion bought in 1944 the former home of A. W. Skinner on Scenic Avenue, which is a major bridge between the Skinner family line and later Legion occupancy

This is important because it supports a later-generation Skinner with the means and local standing to be associated with a significant village residence, while also adding evidence of later financial decline.
